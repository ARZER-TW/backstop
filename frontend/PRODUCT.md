# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Crypto-literate users on a Sui dApp, wallet in hand, with transactional intent:
- **Creators** launching a campaign and locking a refund bonus up front.
- **Backers** browsing campaigns and pledging in a quick decide-and-commit flow, then later claiming refunds.
Context: desktop/laptop, testnet wallet connected. They need to grasp the mechanism (refund + bonus on failure) fast and act with confidence that escrowed funds are safe.

## Product Purpose
Backstop is a Dominant Assurance Contract (DAC) crowdfunding dApp on Sui. It breaks the crowdfunding first-mover problem by making "back a campaign and get *more* back if it fails" a credible, on-chain promise. Success = a user trusts the escrow at a glance and completes launch / pledge / claim without confusion.

## Brand Personality
Precise, calm, credible. Reads like a serious financial instrument, not a web3 toy. The mechanism is clever; the interface is quiet, legible, and confident.

## Anti-references
- Loud web3/crypto aesthetics: neon gradients, glows, animated blobs, gradient text, "degen" energy.
- SaaS clichés: hero-metric template, identical icon-card grids, all-caps tracked eyebrows over every section.
- Anything playful or gamified — this handles money.

## Design Principles
- **Trust through legibility.** Every figure (raised, target, bonus, projected refund) is unambiguous and high-contrast. No number is decorative.
- **Quiet by default, color for meaning.** Neutrals carry the surface; color marks state (funding / succeeded / failed) and the primary action only.
- **The mechanism is the hero.** Surface the "fail → you profit" logic plainly, without decoration.
- **Earned familiarity.** Linear/Vercel-grade standard affordances; no invented controls, no surprise.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG AA: body text ≥4.5:1, large/UI ≥3:1, visible focus rings, `prefers-reduced-motion` honored. Status is never color-only (always paired with a label).
